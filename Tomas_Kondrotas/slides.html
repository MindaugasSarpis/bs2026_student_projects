What is OSRS?

Old School RuneScape is a MMORPG released in 2013 — a restored 2007 snapshot of the original RuneScape.

  • Point-and-click fantasy MMO
  • Active playerbase of 150,000+ concurrent users
  • Skill-based progression across 23 skills
  • In-game currency: gold pieces (gp)
  • Has its own economy, driven entirely by players

The Grand Exchange

The Grand Exchange (GE) is OSRS's central player-driven marketplace — the equivalent of a stock exchange for virtual goods.

  • ~4,500 tradeable items, from runes to rare weapons
  • Players place buy and sell offers at their chosen price
  • Matching engine pairs offers — just like a real order book
  • 4-hour buy limits per item prevent market manipulation
  • Prices fluctuate based purely on supply & demand

Just like a real market

The GE behaves like a real market: booms, crashes, hype cycles, and bubbles.

Famous example: the Partyhat saga

  • Dropped during a 2001 Christmas event
  • No longer obtainable — pure scarcity play
  • Peaked at ~2.1 billion gp (the GE coin cap)
  • Crashed hard in 2018–2019 after duping scandals and bond inflation
  • Behaved exactly like a speculative collectible — think tulips or NFTs

The OSRS Wiki Prices API

Community-run, free, no auth required — the gold standard for GE data.

GET https://prices.runescape.wiki/api/v1/osrs/endpoint
  • /mapping — all tradeable items (name, icon, buy limit, alch value)
  • /latest — current instant buy/sell prices
  • /5m, /1h — rolling averages and trade volumes
  • /timeseries — historical prices (5m / 1h / 6h / 24h, up to 1 year)
Mapping is cached locally for 24 hours (mapping_cache.json) — so we don't hammer the wiki on every page load.

Step 1: Scraping the icons

The API gives item names, but not sprites. The icons live on the OSRS Wiki as PNG files.

  • One-time script: scrape_osrs_icons.py
  • Downloads ~4,500 PNGs from oldschool.runescape.wiki/images/
  • Tries the _detail variant first, falls back to plain name on 404
  • Throttles with time.sleep(0.1) — be polite to the wiki 🙏
Icons are served as static files by Flask. Not checked into git (.gitignore) — users run the scraper on first setup.

The Alchemy Page

High Alchemy is a magic spell that converts an item into coins at a fixed value — one of the game's oldest money-makers.

  • Every item has a built-in high-alch value (comes from the mapping)
  • If buy_price + nature_rune_cost < alch_value, you profit per cast
  • The page shows every item as a sortable table:
      buy price · alch value · profit/loss
  • Green rows = profitable · red rows = loss

Flipping & the Flip Picks page

Flipping = buying low, selling high on the GE — pocketing the spread between instant-buy and instant-sell prices.

What the page shows

  • Top 10 items ranked by a composite score
  • score = margin % × log(volume) × log(max_profit)
  • Rewards margin, liquidity, and profit ceiling (margin × 4h buy limit)
  • Filters out noise: margin ≥ 1%, buy ≥ 100 gp, volume ≥ 10/h
Reality check: I made a handful of flips for ~9,000 gp. Bots and faster players take the best spreads in seconds — as a manual tool it's useful for learning the market, but unreliable as income. Would need full automation to actually be profitable.
